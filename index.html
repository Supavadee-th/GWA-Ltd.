Important Laws and Regulations


Registration: Both the drone and the operator must be registered via the CAAT UAS Portal. Mandatory third-party liability insurance is also required.Flight Operations: A flight permit must be requested. Flights are only permitted from 06:00 AM to 06:00 PM (daylight hours only), at an altitude not exceeding 90 meters, and are prohibited in restricted zones such as airports or urban areas. Penalties: Unauthorized drone use or failure to register may result in imprisonment for up to 1 year, a fine of up to 40,000 Baht, or both.

Choosing an Agricultural Drone

When choosing an agricultural drone, you should consider three main factors: farm size and crop type, such as large drones for large areas, and drones with sensors for rough terrain. Task Type: Different drone models are suited for different tasks, such as spraying, fertilizing, or surveying. You should choose a drone with the right accessories for your specific needs. Drone Features: Consider key features such as water tank capacity, GPS navigation(for accuracy), and obstacle detection sensors(for safety).

Drone Operation and Maintenance

Pre-Flight: Before you fly, inspect the drone's condition, check the propellers and battery, and make sure to update the firmware. This prevents accidents and operational errors.During Operation: Always follow the user manual, mix chemicals safely, and plan the flight path to suit the specific crop. This ensures maximum efficiency. Post-Flight: Clean the drone immediately after spraying chemicals to prevent corrosion. Store the battery at the recommended charge level to extend its lifespan.
